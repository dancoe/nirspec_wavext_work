## Algorithm (identical to v2)

### Step 1 — Derive k(λ)

$$R_i(\lambda) = \frac{S_{\mathrm{obs},i}(\lambda)}{f_{\mathrm{CALSPEC},i}(\lambda)}$$

$$k_i(\lambda) = R_i(\lambda) - \tilde{\alpha}_P(\lambda)\cdot r_{2,i}(\lambda) - \tilde{\beta}_P(\lambda)\cdot r_{3,i}(\lambda)$$

$$k(\lambda) = \mathrm{median}_i\bigl[k_i(\lambda)\bigr], \quad \text{40-channel box-smoothed}$$

**k-sources:** P330E (PID 1538), J1743045 (PID 1536).  
**G191-B2B excluded** from k derivation (hot WD artefact at λ > 2.5 µm).

### Step 2 — α̃(λ), β̃(λ)

Loaded from Parlanti published calibration FITS (same as IFU v3).

### Correction formula

$$f_\mathrm{corr}(\lambda) = \frac{S_\mathrm{obs}(\lambda) - \tilde{\alpha}(\lambda)\,f(\lambda/2) - \tilde{\beta}(\lambda)\,f(\lambda/3)}{k(\lambda)}$$

## Interpretation

**P330E and J1743045** — EXCELLENT agreement within ~5% for both G140M and G235M NRS2 after correction.

**G191-B2B** — G235M corrects well. G140M shows ~20–30% overcorrection at λ > 2.5 µm (hot WD photometric artefact, consistent with IFU v3 result).

**MAST vs L3 ext** — The Spec3-generated NRS2 products clearly demonstrate extended wavelength coverage. After correction, the NRS2 extension aligns with the MAST L3 truth from next-grating observations.

**FS vs IFU k comparison** — FS k values are ~20–30% higher than IFU v3 k values at all wavelengths. This is a real mode-dependent difference: the photometric calibration pipeline applies different normalisation for IFU vs FS modes, so the NRS2 extended region receives a different effective throughput correction. The two sets of k coefficients are therefore NOT interchangeable.
