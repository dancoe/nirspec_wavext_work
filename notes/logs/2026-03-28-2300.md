## 1. Problem Identified: Scaling Mismatch in CAL_COMPONENTS Plot

**Observation**: The `CAL_COMPONENTS_P330E.png` plot showed extended-wavelength observations
(G140M NRS2) were ~10³× brighter than the nominal spectrum (G235M), not overlapping as expected.

**Root causes** (three compounding bugs in `plot_parlanti_components_multi.py`):

### Bug 1 — Unit mismatch
- `G140M_EXT` was reading `data/PID1538_P330E/jw01538160001_06101_00001_nrs2_extract_1d.fits`
  — a **per-exposure NRS2 extract_1d in DN/s**
- `G140M_NOM` was reading the stage3 FS product (in **Jy**)
- Factor: ~10³ between DN/s and Jy

### Bug 2 — Zero wavelength overlap for same-grating comparison
- G140M FS NOM ends at **1.87 µm**; G140M NRS2 EXT starts at **1.96 µm** (no overlap!)
- The script computed `scale = median(NOM_at_ext_wl) / median(EXT_at_ext_wl)` in this window and always got NaN

### Bug 3 — Wrong data source for V3 calibration
- V3 solver (`solve_parlanti_bootstrap_v3.py`) also used per-exposure DN/s NRS2 extracts,
  with an ad-hoc DN/s→Jy rescaling at the boundary
- Resulting k≈0.89 is **not applicable** to the IFU stage3_ext products
