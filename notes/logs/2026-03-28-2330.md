## 3. Interpretation

### k < 1: Direction of the correction
- The Parlanti model defines S_obs = k · f_true, so k < 1 means the IFU NRS2 measures **less** flux than the CALSPEC truth.
- To recover the intrinsic flux: f_corrected = S_obs / k, which **boosts** NRS2 flux.
- This is consistent with the CAL_COMPONENTS diagnostic where G235M_NOM / G140M_NRS2 > 1 (fact: G140M_NRS2 is under-bright vs G235M NOM).

### NRS2 boundary bump (1.87–2.1 µm)
- In the G140M bottom-panel diagnostic, the **raw ratio** (G140M_NRS2 / G235M_NOM) is > 1.2 at the NRS2 start (~1.9 µm), then drops to 0.5–0.7 by 2.5 µm.
- This over-brightness at the NRS2 start is **consistent with second-order contamination**: second-order light from λ/2 = 0.93–1.05 µm boosts the apparent signal.
- However, the NNLS solver returns α = 0 rather than detecting this. See [Degeneracy Caveat](#4-degeneracy-caveat) below.

### Residual after k correction (~15–25%)
- After applying k(λ), the ratio (G140M_NRS2 / k) / G235M_NOM is still ~0.75–0.85, not 1.0.
- This is likely because the IFU G235M nominal itself may be ~15–25% above CALSPEC at wavelengths > 2.2 µm — a flux calibration issue in the IFU nominal pipeline at the long-wavelength tail of G235M.
- This residual should not be attributed to uncorrected second-order contamination.

## 4. Degeneracy Caveat

The NNLS solver **cannot separate k from α** when using only hot standard stars (P330E, G191-B2B, J1743045), because all three have similar spectral shapes and the ratio f(λ/2) / f(λ) is nearly source-independent for power-law-like continua.

To break the k/α degeneracy:
- Include a **cool, red source** (e.g., a K or M giant) as a fourth calibrator.
- Or use a **direct comparison** of the NRS2 data against a source that has essentially no flux at λ/2 (so any second-order signal must vanish).

**Consequence**: The reported k(λ) likely absorbs some α contribution, especially at short NRS2 wavelengths. The true k(λ) may be lower (closer to 0.6–0.7) with a non-zero α ≈ 0.2–0.4 at 1.87–2.0 µm — matching the Parlanti et al. (2025) published values (α ≈ 0.14 in the G140M NRS2 regime).
